Usage with Kohya-sd-script   v260510

To easily use these Emo series with Kohya-sd-script,
simply place this folder as-is into the "sd-scripts" folder within your Kohya-sd-script installation:

sd-script/optimizer

With this setup,

    --optimizer_type=optimizer.emosens.EmoSens
    --optimizer_type=optimizer.emoairy.EmoAiry
    --optimizer_type=optimizer.emocats.EmoCats
    --optimizer_type=optimizer.emotion.EmoTion
    --optimizer_type=optimizer.emovoid.EmoVoid
    
You can utilize each optimizer by simply specifying one of the above.

|||Usage options|||
    ●FFT-mode on：
    fftmode=True
    ●Shadow off:
    use_shadow=False
    ●notify off:
    notify=False
    ●stopcoef (default：0.02):  
    stopcoef=0.02 
    ●eps(Division by zero prevention)：
    eps=1e-8

|||Usage examples|||
    shadow-system: on (option)
    --optimizer_args "use_shadow=True"

    With the Early Stop indicator, no one will ever miss the perfect timing to stop training.
    The following code will display "✨[READY TO STOP]✨" during training (by measuring the reliability of the Loss).
    Once this signal appears frequently, it is a sign that the model has absorbed most of what it can learn (signaling that you can stop training).
    Do not rush to stop immediately; instead, prioritize testing the checkpoints saved just before and after the indicator lights up (the ones closest to the notification).
    (DiT models, due to the fluctuations in Flow-Matching, please treat the checkpoints around a dense cluster of notifications as your top candidates.)

        if self.stop_base >= 0.3 and scale_base_m <= self.stopcoef:
            self.should_stop = True       # 💡 外部からこれを見て判断可
            if self.notify:               # 💡 収束・安定の「お知らせ」
                print(f"✨[READY TO STOP]✨")
        else:
            self.should_stop = False      # 💡 誤判定などの取り消し

    This notification is normally enabled, but you can disable it if you don't need it. 
    You can turn off the notification by setting the argument to “False”.

        notify=True     →   notify=False

    The default value for this coefficient is 0.02, but you can adjust the loss value that indicates convergence (notification) to suit your preferences.
        self.stopcoef
    You can adjust the convergence point to suit your needs, such as creating character LoRAs, creating art style LoRAs, or performing lighter training.
    This feature allows the optimizer to suggest an appropriate step based on the assessment that the loss has stabilized to a certain extent and there is a significant difference between the doubt and confidence levels.
    Please try using the periods before and after the spike in notifications as potential timeframes.
